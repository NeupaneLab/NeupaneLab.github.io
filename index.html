Plant Phylogenetics Lab

Department of Biology, Ball State University

In our lab, we construct evolutionary trees of living organisms, mainly plants, known as phylogenies. We utilize DNA and morphological trait data to build these phylogenies. By analyzing these phylogenies, we investigate the how, where, and when different groups of plants (and animals) have evolved over macroevolutionary timescales. Additionally, we contribute to organizing the vast diversity of living organisms into distinct groups based on their evolutionary relationships, a field known as systematics.

Areas of Research

  • Plant Systematics
  • Phylogenetics Theory & Comparative Methods
  • Bioinformatics

Research

Our research focuses on understanding the evolutionary relationships among plant species using molecular and morphological data. We employ cutting-edge computational methods to reconstruct phylogenetic trees and investigate patterns of diversification across different plant lineages.

Current Projects

We are currently working on several projects investigating the evolution of key plant traits and their correlation with environmental factors. Our work combines field collection, laboratory analysis, and computational biology to address fundamental questions in plant evolution.
