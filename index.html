Welcome to my website!

I am an Assistant Professor of Biology at Ball State University.

In our lab, we construct evolutionary trees of living organisms, mainly plants, known as phylogenies. We utilize DNA and morphological trait data to build these phylogenies. By analyzing these phylogenies, we investigate the how, where, and when different groups of plants (and animals) have evolved over macroevolutionary timescales. Additionally, we contribute to organizing the vast diversity of living organisms into distinct groups based on their evolutionary relationships, a field known as systematics.

Areas of Research

Areas of research in our lab include:

  • Plant Systematics
  • Phylogenetics Theory & Comparative Methods
  • Bioinformatics
  • Macroevolutionary Processes

Research

Understanding and conserving biodiversity is the underlying theme of my research, which primarily involves:

  1. Documenting plant diversity
  2. Studying evolutionary processes that shape the current plant diversity in the tropics

Current Research Projects

Our lab focuses on constructing phylogenies using both molecular and morphological data. We are particularly interested in understanding the evolution of plant groups in tropical regions and their biogeographic patterns.

Methodological Approaches

We incorporate a range of techniques including:

  • DNA sequencing and phylogenomic analyses
  • Morphological character analysis
  • Comparative methods in phylogenetics
  • Biogeographic modeling
